Built for safety

🔐

End-to-End Encryption

Every message is encrypted with the Signal Protocol (X3DH + Double Ratchet). The server never sees plaintext. Even encrypted emoji reactions.

🧅

Tor Routing

Desktop traffic routes through Tor automatically. The server never knows your IP address. Your location stays private.

🔑

Cryptographic Identity

Your Ed25519 keypair IS your identity. No email, no phone, no personal data. Authentication via challenge-response signatures.

🤝

Web of Trust

Invite-only with a vouching system. Trust scores determine capabilities. The community validates itself.

🚨

SOS Alerts

Send geolocated distress signals to nearby trusted members. Three severity levels. Real-time responder tracking.

🗑️

Panic Wipe

One-tap emergency data destruction. Clears keys, messages, Tor data, and all traces from your device instantly.

How it works

1

Get Invited

A trusted member shares a KUU-XXXXXX invite code with you. Kuurier is invite-only — everyone is accountable.

2

Generate Your Key

The app generates an Ed25519 keypair. Your public key is your identity. No personal information is ever collected.

3

Build Trust

Get vouched for by other members. Your trust score grows, unlocking features like posting, creating events, and sending SOS alerts.

4

Communicate Securely

Send encrypted messages, share verified reports, coordinate events, and alert your network — all through Tor.

Why we built this

Activists around the world face surveillance, censorship, and targeted harassment. Mainstream platforms collect personal data, expose IP addresses, and comply with government requests that endanger those fighting for justice.

Kuurier was built because activists deserve tools that protect them by default — not as an afterthought. Every design decision prioritizes the safety of the people using it: encryption that can't be bypassed, identities that can't be traced back, and a network that can't be infiltrated without earning real trust.

We believe that secure communication is a human right, and that the tools activists use should be as resilient as the movements they support.
